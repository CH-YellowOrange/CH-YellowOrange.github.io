Cheng (Aatrox) Huang

I am a second-year Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Computer Science, Southern Methodist University in Dallas, TX 75205 USA. My current research direction is AI for Health care, especially biomarker-based diagnosis and treatment of glaucoma. I am truly grateful to have been mentored by Prof. Karanjit Kooner and Prof. Jui-Kai (Ray) Wang from University of Texas Southwestern Medical Center.

Prior to that, I got my master's degree from the Chinese University of Hong Kong in 2022. At that stage, my research direction was AIoT (Artificial Intelligence of Things) for public health. Before this master, I was a Ph.D. student in the Department of Electronic Engineering, Tsinghua University, advised by Prof. Huazhong Yang and Prof. Lu Zhang. At that time, my research direction was AI chip design and electronic design automation (EDA). I was not particularly interested in hardware, so I left the program to work as a research assistant before pursuing a master's degree. I am deeply grateful to my two supervisors for their strong recommendation letters. And I obtained the bachelor's degree from University of Electronic Science and Technology of China in 2020. During my undergraduate studies, I was incredibly lucky to meet Prof. Yongbin Yu and Prof. Yunbo Rao, who inspired and mentored me in my early research journey. At that time, my research interests were medical imaging and memristor.
